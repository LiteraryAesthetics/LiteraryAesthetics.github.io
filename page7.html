Drabbles

Follow Your Heart

Someone once told me,
"You must follow your heart."

Then I replied,
"If my heart broke into a million pieces,
which piece should I follow then?"

Time Travelled

You've always been that person whom I felt I've seen before; in a different time, maybe even before I was born. Everytime I see you, I felt like we're in a different dimension; a one in the past, a different place, but the same person and the same feelings. I've seen you before. I met you before. I've kissed you before. I loved you before. That time we met at the present world, I knew you were that person. And by that moment we saw the setting skies as the birds fly, a fascinating view, as good as new, all I can think of is you.

Reaching You

"It wasn't that easy to try and reach the one you love.
I did everything I could; to reach you, and let you
know I need you, but no matter how fast the day
and night passes by, I still couldn't get to you.
I suffered, and felt the sorrow. But I never stopped.

Because I love you." The moon told the sun.

The Last Time

This is the last time I will write about you.

This is the last time I'll remember you, standing beside the flickering lights in a soothing dawn. The last time I will picture your sweet simper as you play around the arcade controllers. This is the last time I'll regret I never told you how I felt; the words I could have said, the hands I could have held, the kisses I could have initiated. This is my last confession—and the last time I will tell you this. I did fell for your magic tricks, head over heels, trying to keep my tranquility beneath the breathtaking view of your blissful pulchritude.

This is the last time I will tell you I loved you.


 

A Million Lives

live in this world a million times,

I would have written these
poems over and over,
kiss those lips every once in
a while,

And I would have fallen
for you, still, even for
a million times.

 

The Night

The night isn't a thing to be frightened of.
The night, is just a night. It's dark, and cold, and frightening.
Now stop looking at its flaws. Start seeking for it's beautiful places. Search for its lovely features.
Deal with fear.
And that's when you'll finally realize that the night was just the reason the moon and stars shine, unveiling the beauty of darkness.

Forgive & Forget

     "I'm sorry." She whispered to my ears as her tears slowly
fell down.
    "It's okay. You've been forgiven." I told her and forced my lips to form a smile.
    I told her I'm okay.
    Even though she left.
    She had her biggest regret.     
But sadly, my heart can never forget. 

The Constant Stars

That night when both of us looked at the sparkling bright sky, I said the curious thoughts out for you to hear, wondering if the stars ever rests, and if they can dim their light whenever they want to.                                                                                                                                                                                                              And as you rest your head beside mine, you spoke the words I never expected. "The stars that you can see, they don't shine just because they want to. They can't dim just because they want to rest. They glow because they're destined to, until they can't shine anymore. And me, I am destined too; to love you, and love you until I can't love you no longer."

Kissing Season

Some people say that your first kiss will take you to different places. When you had it, it will feel like the warmth of the first bloomed flower in spring; it is the soothing breeze that touches your skin beneath the shining sun in summer, the leaves that falls one by one that fills the ground with comfort in autumn, or the great beauty of every falling snow that clings to the atmosphere in every first snow in winter.

As for me, a first kiss is when we both found the perfect time for our hands to intertwine as your soft red lips that taste like every season touches this curious, mischievous lips of mine.

Uncompromising Demeanor

There was once a story about a twisted tale—a legend you once heard marked with a noticeable stain. We all thought that the prince looked for her, searching for the perfect fit with the mysterious glass slipper. But did it really happen at that ball? Did she really run away before the clock ticked twelve?

You see, there was a different side of how Cinderella lost her slipper. She did not accidentally leave the glass slipper—she grabbed it, held it, then smashed it onto the prince's head. The prince was a motherfucking arsehole, and she was an all-out boss who beats every hypocrite that dares to grieve and maltreat women. 

Fan Of Heartbreaks

     I am a huge fan of this thing called heartbreaks. It's just that I find it amazing, for how it affects a person.
I love how it broke my heart into million pieces by that time the one I love left me.
I love how it made me cry for four straight weeks-a month-or maybe more than that. I love how it tears me up, and make me feel blue, and lonely and sorrowful.
I love how painful it is. I love how it stays inside and makes you feel like letting go. I love its little imperfections.
But that one thing that I love the most about heartbreaks is how it made me change. Inspired me to stand up and wipe those tears that came from my lonely eyes that's full of pain. I love how it made me something new, stonger, braver, and better.

 

Our Constancy

If two were to rewrite the
story of Snow White,
I wonder if the princess
would still dare to take
a bite from the poisoned
apple just to end up
with the prince.

If we were to rewrite our story,
I'd still take the risks and face
the consequences just to
end up growing old, suffering
and dying with someone as
exceptionally wonderful
as you.

Love Comes

They say love comes from the most unexpected moments, as when the soothing wind faintly blows by your face. It may come slow, and it may swiftly dash between those little flower petals of spring or the falling leaves by autumn.

As for me, love knocked onto my door like this:

The wind blows, drifting and whirling as I walked by. Then before I knew it, I just got dust in my eye. Painful—bearable yet unwanted, like what happens when love takes over. So I closed my eyes, and took the effort to blink as I muster up the courage to tell that I'll get through the pain. Slowly, it faded away and left me an experience that it comes both in painful and joyful ways when you fall in-love.

Paradox

You don't know, but I admired the way your eyes speak for your soul. You were a fascinating paradox-consisting of a mingled sweetness and weirdest catastrophes. Unpredictable as you are, you blend with love and sorrow; you cry but you laugh and your words shake from an agreement to an odd disapproval. However, even though how much you stop and go, I admire the way your eyes speak for your soul. You are full of hope, of strength, of bravery and loveliness. You are a total paradox-but you will be the paradox I won't regret I have been confused and have felt attached with. This is what your name sounds like inside my heart.

My Everything

I've been to the cities of love and walked beneath the vabrant hues of sunlight. I've ran towards the darkest forests and made my way through the night. I've been to many places, but no matter how much happiness lingers around me and no matter how fast I run, my heart says you're still the one.

I can't deny, you are like the enticement that I can't defy. Your sultry eyes keeps me wondering why—I tried to leave you, but why can't I? Your voice, your fragrance is all that I'm missing. My heart can't deny the reason for its beating. Maybe it's possible that a halt was not coming to stop all our loving and fate keeps on reminding that you will always remain my love, my one and only, because to me, you are my everything.

Reader

Books have always been a friend of mine. It gives me inspirations, fear, warmth, happiness—all of it. It gives me freedom to go to different places; from Korea to Canada or either France or China, it lets me explore the world I'm living in.

And it reminds me of you.

Of how your fingers trace every page,
the way your eyes glimmer in every letter,
the ardor of sailing towards the unknown;
a world we knew no one could measure.

I would keep on reading the next chapters, after all, our story won't be drawing to an epilogue any moment.

Your World

The glances you take,
the movements you make,
your captivating eyes,
your sun-bright smile,
your world, my dear,
I became a victim;
I'm now trapped in
this world called
you.

Empathy

People always asks me,
        "How are you able to write things you
        never experienced?"

Then I'll respond,
        "Must your heart be crushed and torn out
        from your body to say you've felt being
        heartbroken?

        Likewise,
        Must a judge experience killing someone
        to convict a person of murder?"
